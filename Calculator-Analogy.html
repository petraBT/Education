The Printing Press Analogy

Around 1440, the printing press democratized knowledge, shifting education from memorization to synthesis.

üìú

The Initial Fear

Scholars worried about misinformation and that common people couldn't be trusted to interpret texts for themselves.

The Educational Shift

Education shifted from rote memorization of a few rare texts to the critical skill of reading, comparing, and synthesizing many sources.

The Outcome
